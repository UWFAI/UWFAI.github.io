AIRG presents:

Dr. Ken Ford

Toward Cognitive Orthotics:
A Human-Centered future for AI

Tuesday, March 24, 2015 Bldg 4, rm 102 5:30 - 7:00 PM

The emerging concept of human-centered (HCC) computing represents a significant shift in thinking about intelligent machines and, indeed, about information technology in general. Human-centered computing embodies a "systems view," in which human thought and action and technological systems are seen as inextricably linked and equally important aspects of analysis, design, and evaluation. From an AI perspective, the HCC framework is focused less on stand-alone exemplars of computational cognitive talent, and is concerned more with computational systems designed to amplify human cognitive and perceptual abilities. The human-centered approach results in systems that can be regarded as cognitive orthoses, much as eyeglasses are a sort of ocular orthoses. The design and fit of computational and electromechanical orthoses require a broader interdisciplinary range than is typically found in one organization, and IHMC staff includes computer scientists, engineers, cognitive psychologists, neuroscientists, physicians, linguists, philosophers, and social scientists of various stripes, as well as some people who resist all attempts to classify them.

About the AI Research Group

The University of West Florida Artificial Intelligence Research Group is a student run organization dedicating to AI research and advocacy. The group is open to all UWF students regardless of major or experience. Meetings are held every Thursday in the AI lab, room 148, building 4.

AI Lab

The AI Research Group meets and works in the AI & Project Lab in the SSE building 4, room 148.
The lab has 6 highly-capable all-in-one desktops, multiple cabinets for storage, a large overhead monitor for presentations, and several workbenches. The lab is also furnished with two iRobot Create robots, a LEGO Mindstorms kit, and a number of IntelliBrain-Bot Deluxe robots.

Current Projects

The AI group is currently working on a competitive gaming environment where AI agents battle over resources. We have begun to form subgroups which will design their own Intelligent Agents which will compete in the environment for ultimate glory. Groups are planning to implement various Machine Learning, Neural Networks, Genetic, Evolutionary and Search Algorithms to achieve optimal performance within the environment. Further down the line we hope to simulate the game environment in the real world using various robots. The group will present the current state of the game environment at the UWF Student Scholars Symposium in April.

Get Involved

We meet once a week to work on projects, learn about AI topics and design the future and direction of the group. Whether you are passionate about the field of AI or just curious to learn more, we would love to have you.
